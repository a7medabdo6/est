What is the EST?

The Egyptian Scholastic Test (EST) is developed by experts in 5 continents and validated by experts in the USA. It is developed especially for Egyptian students in the American Diploma division. The EST is a computer-based test that measures the readiness of the students to enter university and assesses the level of students in Reading, Writing and Math.

Who delivers the EST?

The test is created by experts across 5 continents and validated by the EST Board in the USA. It is designed specifically to measure the skills and knowledge acquired by students in the American education system in Egypt against common core standards.

It is a computer-based test delivered in collaboration with Pearson.

The EST seeks to embody and implement the most sophisticated, up-to-date assessment with a suite of assessments that includes EST I and EST II.

What is the difference between the EST I and the EST II?

The EST I is designed to enable all American Diploma students to apply for Egyptian universities, while the EST II is required by certain colleges (practical colleges like the Faculty of Medicine & Engineering) to test skills in Science and Math subjects (Math level 1, Math level 2, Biology, Physics, Chemistry).

The EST I includes three mandatory sections: Reading, Writing and Language, and Math.

The EST I tackles communication skills, comprehension, critical and logical reasoning; all these are basic requirements for a university student. The EST I also includes an optional essay section measuring students' language writing skills.

The EST II is a subject-based test in Science and Math. The EST subject tests cover Math, Biology, Physics, and Chemistry, with each subject test taken separately.

The EST II measures basic scientific knowledge, scientific reasoning, and higher-order thinking skills, proving the abilities and capabilities of the student to pursue education in specific majors related to engineering, science and health care. The choice of subject tests depends on the majors the students are applying for.

Why do I need to take the EST?

The Egyptian Ministry of Higher Education has set guidelines for admission to universities with the American High School Diploma which state that students should take the EST I in order to be admitted to public or national universities and institutions. For some majors, students should take both the EST I and EST II.

How can I study for the EST? What sources can I use?

The main study materials needed to prepare for the EST is the curriculum material which is taught in the American Diploma schools.

The student does not need private lessons. The school and the school teachers can provide the student with all the learning opportunities needed to get excellent EST grades.

The students can also check sample EST tests using the following links:

  • Previous papers for the EST I and the EST II

  • Links to material which fits the EST and is FREE and available globally on the internet

It is worth noting that the official EST scoring does not penalize wrong answers. In other words, the total raw score is the sum of the number of questions answered correctly.

How are the exams scored?

The EST exams are fully electronic. The exams are authored by experts from around the world and reviewed by a board of experts based in the USA. Once the tests are administered, the scores are assigned automatically to each item to produce the student scores, with the exception of the optional essay which is scored manually. These scores are then reviewed to apply the psychometric analysis by the EST Board of Experts before the results are issued.

What is the EST test structure?

The EST assesses the student’s knowledge of the American Diploma program and the exams are structured as follows:

EST I:

– Literacy Test 1 (Writing and Language): 35 minutes - 44 multiple choice questions

– Literacy Test 2 (Reading): 65 minutes - 52 multiple choice questions

– Math: 90 minutes - 45 multiple choice questions and 13 short constructive response questions (Graphing or scientific calculator is allowed for some questions)

– Essay (Optional): 50 minutes

EST II:

– Math Level 1: 60 minutes - 50 multiple choice questions (Graphing or scientific calculator is allowed for part 2 of the exam)

– Biology: 60 minutes - 80 multiple choice questions (Calculator is NOT allowed)

– Physics: 60 minutes - 75 multiple choice questions (Calculator is NOT allowed)

– Chemistry: 60 minutes - 85 multiple choice questions (Calculator is NOT allowed)

– Math Level 2: 60 minutes - 50 multiple choice questions (Graphing or scientific calculator is allowed)

How can American Diploma students who are studying abroad, but want to apply for Egyptian universities, take the EST exams?

At the moment the exams can be taken only in Egypt. The EST Board is expanding its operations to support Egyptian students abroad, and will announce all updates on the EST website.

Who should take the EST II?

A holder of an American High School Diploma who wants to apply to the faculties or institutes of Medicine, Pharmacy, Dentistry, Physiotherapy, Veterinary Medicine, Nursing, Engineering, Information Technology, Computer, Fine Arts (Architecture section), and Urban Planning, has to take two subjects in EST II, in addition to EST I.

For all other majors, only EST I is enough.

Which subjects should I take in the EST II?

For the faculties or institutes of Medicine, Pharmacy, Dentistry, Physiotherapy, Veterinary Medicine, and Nursing, the two subjects required are Biology and one of the following: Mathematics 1, Physics, Chemistry, or Mathematics 2.

For the faculties or institutes of Engineering, Information Technology, Computer, Fine Arts (Architecture section), and Urban Planning, the two subjects required are Math Level 1 or Math Level 2 and one of the following: Physics, Chemistry or Biology.

Will the EST allow me to apply to universities abroad?

The EST is an Egyptian entrance exam for Egyptian universities. Its results cannot be used for applications to non-Egyptian universities until now. In the vast majority of cases, the American Diploma students are applying for an Egyptian university. But the EST Board is still working with international bodies to get the EST certification recognized internationally.

How long does it take to issue the EST results?

The results are usually issued within two weeks of the exam. The students are emailed their unofficial results. If they require the certified report, students are given the opportunity to order any score report (including older ones) for a limited time after issuing the scores of each trial.

What is the validity of the EST?

The EST I and EST II scores can be used for up to 3 years after the test results, although students need to refer to the Supreme Council of Universities guidelines to confirm the timelines for applying to schools.

How can I get an official certified copy of my score report?

Students do not need to certify a trial directly after its scores are out. They can wait until they take all the trials that they want and then order a certified copy of their selected score report.

Students are given the opportunity to order any score report (including older ones) for a limited time after issuing the scores of each trial.

To order a report, please access your EST account and visit the "Services" link in the Student Dashboard.

If you have not created an account on the new website which we launched in May 2021, please create an account using the same email that you used in your previous attempts. The data in your current EST account is what will appear in your report, so make sure it is accurate and complete.

When you select each report, please specify the email address that you used for that exam even if it matches the email address on the account. Payments are non-refundable and non-transferable. If you select a wrong report or do not indicate the correct email address, you risk losing the payment.

After the deadline for ordering the official score report is over, reports are printed and sent to the Ministry of Education and Technical Education for certification.

Once they are certified, they are shipped by courier to students who are then notified by email with the instructions to track their packages. Students need to ensure that they have included the full information for proper delivery. Failure to deliver the package results in returning it to our office without a refund. More information about ordering a certified report can be found here:

When do I get my Admission Ticket?

After the trial registration deadline, a test center is assigned to each student based on their selection of the governorate and test district and subject to availability.

Students are then contacted by email to view their Admission Tickets in their Student Dashboard and print them to bring them to the test.

To view the Admission Ticket:

  1. Visit https://www.egyptianscholastictest.com
  2. Log in using your email address and the password you set when you created the account. If you forgot the password, click on "Forgot Password" then enter your email address and click "Send". You will receive an email from "registration@egyptianscholastictest.com" with a password reset link. You can then set a new password and log into your account.
  3. In your Dashboard, under "My Tests", access the current session
  4. You will then view your tests and the center, room, and address
  5. Click on EST I Admission Ticket or EST II Admission Ticket
  6. The system will generate a PDF Admission Ticket and will prompt you to save it
  7. Print that file and bring it with you to the test center. You can also save it and send it to someone to print it on your behalf.

Once centers are assigned, they cannot be changed.

What is the payment method for the EST?

Students can only pay online using a credit card which supports 3D security (ability to send a one-time password by SMS to confirm that you authorize the payment). If the card does not support 3D security, payment might fail and the student needs to contact the bank to check for other options.

Will you send my scores to universities?

While we ask students to indicate the universities they plan to apply to, this is currently for statistical purposes only. At the time being, students still have to share their official score report with the university of their choice.

What documents do I need to apply for the EST?

Egyptian students need to input the number of their National ID and upload a colored copy of their ID in their test application.

Students whose National ID has not been issued yet can upload a colored copy of their passport or birth certificate in the National ID field. They will still have to input the number of the National ID in its correct field though.

Non-Egyptian students need to input the number of their passport and upload a colored copy of the passport in their test application.

All students should bring their Admission Ticket and a copy of an identification document that contains a photo (National ID or passport). Birth certificates are not accepted for admission to the test center.

Why am I unable to log into my EST account although I have already taken the EST in the past?

We launched the new website (https://www.egyptianscholastictest.com) with the May 2021 trials, and did not merge the old accounts from the previous trials into it. So if you have not registered for any trial starting May, your old credentials will not work and you will have to create a new account in order to register for any EST session starting May 2021 or order previous score reports.

You can use the email address that you used for a previous trial, and email us to merge older scores into the new account.

Why am I unable to view my old scores in my account?

We have merged the old accounts into the new website for all students who registered by September 2021. If you have registered after September 2021, please email us indicating the previous rounds (and the email used if different from the one on the new website), and we will merge the scores of trials taken before the launch of the new website in May 2021.

I have taken the EST using different email addresses. Can I combine them for matching?

While matching is applicable for the EST, we do not issue a single report that combines the scores from different trials. Instead, students order the certified reports of both trials and submit them to the university which will then apply superscoring when assessing the student's application at its discretion.

The email is not listed on the score report. It is the name and ID or passport number that need to be the same for both reports. So to match between two trials taken under different emails, the student can simply order the official score reports for both trials and submit them to the university.

Which universities acknowledge the EST?

All national Egyptian universities acknowledge the EST. For private universities, please check directly with the university to which you plan to apply.

Do EST trials count for students in Grade 10?

EST trials count for students in Grades 11 and 12. However, as a test institution, we have no restrictions for entering trials even for students who are in Grade 10, as they can enter EST trials as a form of practice.

As for the first counted trial, you will have to check with the Ministry of Education and the Supreme Council of Universities to check which trial would be the first counted EST trial for you.
